What is Uncle Joe's

Uncle Joe’s Peer Counseling and Resource Center is one of WashU’s premier peer counseling resources. We are made up of highly trained undergraduate peers that have undergone over 100+ hours of training on a wide variety of topics.

For more information, check out our website

What Am I Applying For?

You are applying to be a part of Uncle Joe’s and the Uncle Joe’s 2022-2023 Training Class! Applicants that are accepted must complete training the following semester (Spring 2023) to become official Uncle Joe’s Counselors.

Training typically occurs over 10 Saturdays from 10 am-5 pm and 10 Tuesdays from 7 pm-10 pm. We ask that all applicants make sure they are prepared for a similar time commitment. This includes, but is not limited to, not scheduling classes/extracurriculars during those times, not planning family vacations around those times, and ensuring that you are prepared mentally and physically to make that time commitment. We make exceptions for exams, medical emergencies, and other special circumstances.

Last year, the first two weeks of training occurred online due to COVID restrictions, and the rest of the training occurred in person. The modality of next year’s training will depend on COVID restrictions on campus, but we hope to have in-person training.

Am I Eligible to Apply?

The following is not an exhaustive list of things that make an applicant eligible or ineligible for admission into Uncle Joe’s. If you have any specific questions, please reach out to the recruitment email.

Eligibility to Apply for Uncle Joe's 2022-2023 Training Class
  • You are a first year, second year, or third year living on campus/near campus
  • You are an active student this semester taking classes
  • You are attending WashU Undergrad for a minimum of two undergraduate semesters following training
  • You have plans to return to campus/STL next semester if safety permits it
Ineligibility to Apply for Uncle Joe's 2022-2023 Training Class
  • You do not intend to return to campus next semester (due to study abroad, gap semester, etc)
  • You are currently taking a gap semester

Commitments of Uncle Joe's

  • Joe's should be willing to commit to being active in the organization for as many semesters as possible. This entails:
    • Taking phone duties and office duties
    • Attending weekly GBM
    • Assisting with recruitment/outreach events
    • Giving mock interactions to trainees during Tuesday/Saturday training
  • As a volunteer organization we are entirely made up of students, and we need dedicated Joe's to help keep our organization sustainable. Mant Joe's spend at least one semester on our executive board to lead internal organization, collaborate with outer groups for external events, or organize their own projects to aid the organization.

What Does the Application Process Look Like?

The application process requires no counseling experience or knowledge and is open to anyone that meets our eligibility requirements. There is no one “type” of Joe. We encourage anyone who is interested to apply!

  • First Stage: Written Applicatoin due September 23rd
  • Second Stage: First Round Individual Interviews (phone/virtual)
  • Third Stage: Second Round Group Interviews (in-person)

Following each stage, you will be notified individually to schedule your interview if you have moved on to the next round.

Applicants will be alerted before Thanksgiving Break if they have been accepted into the Uncle Joe's’ 2022-2023 Training Class.

Our application process can be competitive and rigorous. Many of our current Joe’s did not get accepted the first time they applied. On the flip side, many of our current Joe’s applied at the last minute and were humbly surprised to be accepted. Everyone’s journey looks different. If you are interested in joining our organization, we encourage you to apply regardless of whether this is your first, second, or third time.
